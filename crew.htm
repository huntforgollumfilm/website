A large number of talented volunteers and contributors make up the crew
of The Hunt For Gollum. This project is a labour of love that would
not be possible without their hard work, skill,
enthusiasm and extreme dedication.

  

 

Director & Exec Producer
CHRIS BOUCHARD
Chris is an British film maker with a growing body of cinematic work. He discovered his passion for film as a student at the University of York Film making Society. He was initially fascinated by the relationship between sound and picture, and studied film music in his early years, then worked at Dolby Laboratories and eventually as a visual effects engineer for a Soho post house. His fascination for cinematic story telling and the intricate dependency between actor and camera drove him to continue to push low budget film-making techniques to realise his vision, often using digital tricks to achieve difficult visual effects. He set out to make The Hunt For Gollum as a tribute to the work of J.R.R. Tolkien and the films of Peter Jackson. His other work includes shorts Disconnect, The Machine, Clone and the apocalyptic cinema project Human Residue.

THE PRODUCTION TEAM

Co-Producer
BRIAN LAVERY
 Brian's experience spans numerous independent film and theatre projects in practically every type of crew role. Hearing about The Hunt For Gollum, he was excited to be part of such an ambitious production with such a low budget, particularly a fan film for a trilogy he loves. As co-producer Brian's responsibilities are on co-ordinating the fight training so the orcs and rangers are ready to do battle, and overseeing prosthetics so everything looks top quality. Aside from producing on this project, Brian is also a director currently working on a music video for UnderOver The Clouds in collaboration with music producer Microwave. Brian is delighted to be part of such an impressive team.

Co-Producer
GLADYS SAN JUAN
Gladys's multinational film experience began in the States with various independent film projects on the West Coast. She was a two time winner of the University of Miami Student Photography Contest and proceeded in working as a Visual Anthropologist where she worked on research in Havana, Cuba. While in London she completed an MA in Social Anthropology from Goldsmith’s College and a Diploma in Film Studies and Screenwriting from Birbeck College she's recently worked on productions presented at the BFI National Film Theatre in London Southbank. Gladys feels this is an excellent and inspiring film project with an outstanding crew, cast and vision. Her role for The Hunt for Gollum has been in production coordination and management, above all supervising the crew as well as being an active supervisor of the art department.
> email: gladys "@" hunt4gollum.com


Co-Producer
JULIANNE HONEY-MENNAL
Julianne’s experience spans many different disciplines. She began as a writer, actor, then director - treading the boards at Rose Bruford Drama school and the West End before deciding that behind the scenes is more fun after all. In 2005 she became Art Director and Set Dresser for photographer partner Richard Paris. As a hardcore Tolkien fan she was very excited to become a part of The Hunt crew. As Co-Producer for wardrobe, Julianne is responsible for the fantastic team of designers and costume makers that give TH4G it’s realism. She is also acting as Script Advisor on the project, and is determined to introduce elves wherever she can!
> email: jul "@" hunt4gollum.com

Co-Producer
SPENCER DURU

Editor
LEWIS ALBROW
Lewis read The Hobbit when he was 7 and LOTR when he was 11 (although he skipped past much of The Council of Elrond...) To be helping to now create part of that world is a privileged chapter of an editing career covering drama, documentaries, music videos and commercials. When not cutting furiously for the BBC or ITV he's getting buried in a wide mix of features which so far include a gangster movie, an Ealing-style comedy, a vampire flick, a record store rom-com and a 'hip hop opera'. He specialised in editing during his last year at sunny seaside Hull after falling in love with the process. Before long he was cutting his teeth in the corporate world with fast turnaround show after show until eventually going freelance to cut his first feature on the G5 on his bedroom. He only wishes he could work on TH4G full-time rather than extra-curricular as it's infinitely more rewarding than daytime BBC. Ah, maybe someday...
And The Hobbit is still his favourite book.
